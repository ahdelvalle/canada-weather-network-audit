About This Audit

This independent audit was conducted to assess the reliability and operational status of Environment Canada's weather station network across Ontario and Quebec. The findings reveal systematic infrastructure failures that compromise the accuracy and availability of critical meteorological data used for:

  • Public weather forecasting and warnings
  • Aviation safety and operations
  • Agricultural planning and climate research
  • Emergency preparedness and disaster response
  • Long-term climate change monitoring

Methodology

The audit employed automated testing protocols to verify:

  • Data Availability: Recent observations within expected timeframes
  • Data Completeness: Presence of essential meteorological parameters
  • Data Quality: Validation against statistical thresholds and physical constraints
  • System Reliability: API response times and error rates

Each station received a comprehensive quality score based on multiple performance indicators. Stations scoring below 70% are classified as degraded; those below 40% are classified as failed.

Key Findings

52.2% of audited stations (12 out of 23) are currently experiencing failures or significant degradation in data quality and availability. This represents a critical gap in Canada's meteorological infrastructure that affects:

  • Real-time weather monitoring across major population centers
  • Historical climate data continuity and reliability
  • Public trust in government weather services
  • Canada's contribution to international meteorological cooperation

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What defines a "failed" weather station?

A station is classified as failed when its quality score falls below 40%, indicating critical issues with data availability, completeness, or accuracy. This typically means the station is not providing timely observations, has significant data gaps, or reports physically implausible values.

Q: How often is this data updated?

The audit was conducted in November 2025. Updates will be published as new audits are performed. Check the "Last Updated" timestamp at the top of the page and in the repository commit history.

Q: Can I verify these findings independently?

Yes! All data sources are publicly accessible through Environment Canada's official portals. The complete audit methodology and source code are available in the GitHub repository. You can replicate the entire analysis using the provided tools and documentation.

Q: What is the significance of a 52.2% failure rate?

A failure rate exceeding 50% indicates systematic infrastructure problems, not isolated incidents. This level of degradation compromises the reliability of weather forecasting, climate monitoring, and public safety systems that depend on accurate meteorological data.
